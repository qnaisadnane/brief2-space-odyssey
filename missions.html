Space Missions

Apollo 11

Agency: NASA (USA)

Goal: First crewed mission to land humans on the Moon and return them safely to Earth.

Launch Date: July 16, 1969

Voyager 1

Agency: NASA (USA)

Goal: Study the outer Solar System (Jupiter, Saturn) and interstellar space.

Launch Date: September 5, 1977

Hubble Space Telescope

Agency: NASA & ESA (USA/Europe)

Goal: Space-based observatory to capture deep-space images and study galaxies, stars, and planets.

Launch Date: April 24, 1990

Mars Pathfinder

Agency: NASA (USA)

Goal: Demonstrate a low-cost way to deliver a lander and rover to Mars, study Martian geology and atmosphere.

Launch Date: December 4, 1996

Curiosity Rover

Agency: NASA (USA)

Goal: Explore Gale Crater on Mars to study habitability and search for signs of microbial life.

Launch Date:November 26, 2011

Rosetta

Agency:ESA (Europe)

Goal: First mission to orbit and land a probe (Philae) on a comet (67P/Churyumov–Gerasimenko).

Launch Date: March 2, 2004

James Webb Space Telescope

Agency:NASA & ESA (USA/Europe)

Goal: Successor to Hubble; study the early universe, exoplanets, and cosmic formation.

Launch Date: April 24, 1990

Artemis I

Agency:NASA (USA)

Goal:Uncrewed test flight around the Moon as part of the Artemis program to return humans to the Moon.

Launch Date:November 16, 2022

Chang’e 4

Agency:CNSA (China)

Goal: First mission to successfully land on the far side of the Moon, carrying rover Yutu-2.

Launch Date:December 7, 2018

Perseverance Rover

Agency:NASA (USA)

Goal:Search for signs of ancient life on Mars, collect rock samples, and test technologies for future missions.

Launch Date:July 30, 2020
